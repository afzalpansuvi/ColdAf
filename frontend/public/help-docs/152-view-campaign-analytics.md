# How to View Campaign Analytics in ColdAF

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 152

Knowing how your campaigns perform is the foundation of better outreach. If you need a campaign analytics coldaf dashboard that shows real numbers, the Analytics section gives you everything in one place. Tracking cold email campaign metrics like delivery, open, and reply rates helps you decide what to scale and what to fix. A strong email campaign performance dashboard lets you move beyond gut feeling and base decisions on data. ColdAF breaks down every campaign by volume, engagement, and trend so you can spot winners early and cut losers fast.

The analytics update in near real time, which means you can check on a campaign shortly after a send batch completes. You can also zoom into specific date ranges to see how a launch week performed, or compare a campaign against its own historical data. This is especially useful for agencies managing multiple client accounts under one organization, since each campaign's data is scoped clearly and easy to export.

## You can view campaign analytics in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. Go to **Analytics** from the main navigation menu.
2. Select the **Campaigns** tab at the top of the analytics page.
3. Choose the campaign you want to review from the dropdown list.
4. Select a date range: **7d**, **30d**, or **90d** to match the period you care about.
5. Review the summary metrics: sent, delivered, opened, clicked, replied, bounced, and unsubscribed.
6. Check the timeline chart to see email volume and engagement patterns over time.
7. Scroll down to the per-lead breakdown table to see individual recipient activity.
8. Toggle the previous period comparison if you want to see how this period stacks up against the last one.

That's it! Now you can review campaign performance and decide what to optimize next.

## Notes

- Data is real-time with a typical delay of one to two minutes after events occur.
- Open rates are calculated from unique opens, so multiple opens by the same person count once.
- Reply rate counts any reply, whether it is positive, negative, or neutral.
- Bounce rate should stay under 2%; higher rates may indicate list quality or SMTP issues.
- Click tracking requires that you include links in your emails; plain text without links will show zero clicks.
- Use the date range to zoom in on specific launch windows or troubleshooting periods.
- Export data to CSV for deeper analysis in your own spreadsheet or BI tools.
- Per-lead data is available for the selected date range only; older data is summarized at the campaign level.
