# How to Warm Up an Email Account in ColdAF

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 156

Building a strong sender reputation is critical before you launch any serious cold outreach. If you start blasting emails from a brand-new or recently purchased account, ISPs will flag you as suspicious and your messages will land in spam. Learning how to **warm up an email account** properly is the foundation of successful cold email deliverability. Whether you are preparing a single address or scaling up a multi-brand operation, using the right **email warmup** strategy inside ColdAF protects your domain health and ensures your messages reach the inbox. This guide walks you through setting up a structured warmup campaign so you can ramp volume safely without risking your sender reputation.

## You can warm up an email account in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. Go to the **Warmup** page from the main navigation.
2. Click **Create Warmup Campaign**.
3. Select an existing **Warmup Pool** or create a new one to group your accounts.
4. Choose your **Ramp Strategy**: select **Linear** for a fixed daily increase, or **Exponential** for a percentage-based increase.
5. Set your start volume. For example, enter **5 emails/day** as the starting point.
6. Set your target volume. For example, enter **50 emails/day** as the goal.
7. Set the duration. For example, enter **30 days** to complete the ramp period.
8. Enable **Engagement Simulation** so ColdAF simulates realistic opens and replies.
9. Click **Start Warmup** to launch the campaign.

That's it! Now you can ramp your email volume safely while building a positive sender reputation with ISPs.

## Notes

- **Warmup** builds sender reputation gradually by increasing volume and simulating engagement over time.
- **Linear** ramp means your daily volume increases by a fixed number of emails each day.
- **Exponential** ramp means your volume grows by a percentage, making early increases smaller and later ones larger.
- Start with **5-10 emails per day** and ramp to **40-50 per day** over **2-4 weeks** for most new accounts.
- Keep your warmup running alongside real campaigns so your account maintains consistent activity.
- **Engagement Simulation** makes your account activity look natural to ISPs by mimicking real user behavior.
- **Warmup Pools** group accounts together for collective reputation building, which is especially useful for agencies.
