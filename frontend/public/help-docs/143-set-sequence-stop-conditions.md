# How to Set Stop Conditions for Sequences in ColdAF

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 143

Sending emails to leads who have already replied, unsubscribed, or bounced is bad for your reputation and annoying for your prospects. Setting up **sequence stop conditions coldaf** ensures your automation knows when to quit. This guide covers how to configure **auto stop cold email sequence** rules so your outreach stays respectful and compliant. Learning how to set **sequence end rules cold outreach** properly protects your deliverability and keeps your brand professional. Stop conditions are a critical part of list hygiene and help you avoid sending unnecessary follow-ups to people who are no longer viable prospects.

## You can set stop conditions for sequences in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. Go to **Sequence Builder** from the main navigation.
2. Select the sequence you want to edit, or create a new one.
3. Scroll down to the **Stop Conditions** section.
4. Check **Stop on Reply** to automatically stop the sequence when a lead sends a reply.
5. Check **Stop on Unsubscribe** to stop the sequence if the lead clicks an unsubscribe link.
6. Check **Stop on Bounce** to stop the sequence if the email address bounces.
7. Check **Max Steps** to set a hard limit on total steps, for example, **max 4** emails total.
8. Click **Save** to apply the stop conditions to the sequence.

That's it! Now you can set **sequence stop conditions coldaf** and let your sequences end automatically at the right moment.

## Notes

- Stop conditions protect your sender reputation and respect the lead's time and preferences.
- **Stop on Reply** is the most common rule. Once someone responds, you should take over manually instead of continuing automated emails.
- **Stop on Unsubscribe** is required for CAN-SPAM compliance and general email etiquette.
- **Stop on Bounce** prevents you from sending to invalid addresses repeatedly, which damages your domain reputation.
- **Max Steps** caps the sequence even if none of the other stop triggers occur. This prevents sequences from running indefinitely.
- You can combine multiple stop conditions. If any one triggers, the sequence stops for that lead.
