# How to View SMTP Performance Analytics

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 155

Your SMTP accounts are the engine behind every email you send. If you need smtp performance analytics coldaf data to keep that engine healthy, the SMTP Performance tab shows exactly how each account is performing. Tracking email server performance cold email metrics per account helps you spot blacklists, reputation issues, or bad lists before they spread. Detailed smtp stats cold outreach breakdowns let you compare providers, domains, and individual accounts so you can route campaigns to your best performers. ColdAF updates these numbers in real time so you can react quickly.

Not all SMTP accounts are equal. One might have a 95 percent delivery rate while another struggles at 70 percent. Without per-account visibility, you would keep sending through the weaker account and hurt your overall results. The SMTP Performance view makes it easy to identify the top performers and rotate campaigns accordingly. It also helps you decide which accounts need more warmup time or lower daily volume.

## You can view SMTP performance analytics in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. Go to **Analytics** from the main navigation menu.
2. Click the **SMTP Performance** tab at the top of the analytics page.
3. Select the SMTP account you want to review from the dropdown list.
4. Review the sent count and delivery rate for that account.
5. Check the open rate and reply rate per account to see how engaged recipients are.
6. Review the bounce rate and spam complaint rate to catch reputation problems early.
7. Check the daily send volume chart to see if throttling or volume spikes correlate with performance drops.
8. Use the side-by-side view to compare multiple accounts at once.
9. Identify underperforming accounts by looking for red metrics or low delivery rates.

That's it! Now you can monitor account health and make routing decisions based on real data.

## Notes

- Per-account performance reveals which SMTP accounts are healthy and which need attention.
- Low delivery usually means connection issues, blacklisting, or poor IP reputation.
- Low open rate on a specific account suggests poor sender reputation or deliverability problems.
- High bounce rate on one account may indicate a bad list or a configuration issue.
- Compare accounts to find your best performers and route high-value campaigns to them.
- Rotate campaigns toward better-performing accounts to improve overall results.
- Performance data helps you decide which accounts need longer warmup or lower daily volume.
- If an account shows consistent red metrics, consider pausing it and investigating the root cause.
