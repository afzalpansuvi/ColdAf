# How to Monitor Warmup Progress in ColdAF

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 158

Starting a warmup campaign is only half the battle. To protect your deliverability, you need to know whether your accounts are actually ramping on schedule, whether engagement looks healthy, and whether any account needs attention. Monitoring **warmup stats** inside ColdAF gives you a clear view of daily progress, inbox placement estimates, and account health indicators. Tracking **email warmup** progress closely helps you catch problems early, adjust settings mid-campaign, and avoid wasted effort on accounts that are stuck or blocked. This guide shows you how to read your warmup dashboard and take action when something looks off.

## You can monitor warmup progress in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. Go to the **Warmup** page from the main navigation.
2. Select the campaign you want to review from the list.
3. Open the **Progress** tab to see the detailed overview.
4. Review the daily sent count and compare target volume versus actual volume.
5. Check the engagement rate, including opens and replies, to confirm healthy activity.
6. Review the inbox placement estimate, which is based on engagement signals.
7. Check the **Timeline** chart to see volume trends over days.
8. Review the **Account Health** indicators for each account in the campaign.
9. Check if any account shows a red status and needs attention.
10. Click **Adjust Settings** if you need to change volume, duration, or strategy mid-campaign.

That's it! Now you can track your warmup health and make informed adjustments to stay on course.

## Notes

- The **Progress** tab shows your daily ramp progress compared to your original plan.
- Green status means the account is on track and ramping as expected.
- Yellow status means the account is slightly behind schedule but still warming.
- Red status means the account has stopped or is blocked and needs immediate attention.
- The inbox placement estimate is calculated based on engagement signals such as opens and replies.
- A healthy warmup should show an engagement rate between **30% and 60%**.
- If an account turns red, check **SMTP Health** or pause the campaign to investigate before continuing.
- You can adjust settings mid-campaign without restarting the entire warmup process.
