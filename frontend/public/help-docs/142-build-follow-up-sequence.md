# How to Build a Follow-Up Sequence in ColdAF

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 142

Most cold email replies do not come from the first message. They come from the third, fourth, or fifth thoughtful follow-up. Learning how to **build follow up sequence coldaf** is one of the highest-impact skills for improving reply rates. The ColdAF **cold email sequence builder** lets you design multi-step outreach flows with precise delays, so you can **create email sequence cold outreach** campaigns that stay persistent without being pushy. A well-built sequence automates your follow-up rhythm while keeping every message personal and relevant.

## You can build a follow-up sequence in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. Go to **Sequence Builder** from the main navigation.
2. Click **Create Sequence** to start a new sequence.
3. Name your sequence so you can easily identify it later, for example, **SaaS Founder Outreach**.
4. Click **Add Step** to create the first follow-up email.
5. Set the delay for this step, for example, **3 days after previous**.
6. Write the email body for this step, or select a prebuilt template from your library.
7. Click **Add Step** again to create a second follow-up.
8. Set the delay for this step, for example, **7 days after previous**.
9. Write a different angle or value add for this follow-up so it does not repeat the first message.
10. Set stop conditions to prevent over-messaging, such as reply, unsubscribe, or bounce.
11. Click **Save Sequence** to finalize and store the sequence for use in campaigns.

That's it! Now you can **build follow up sequence coldaf** and automate your entire follow-up strategy.

## Notes

- Sequences are reusable across multiple campaigns, so you only need to build them once.
- Each step should add new value. Do not simply say **bumping this** or **following up**.
- Recommended follow-up cadence is **3, 7, 14, and 21 days** after the initial email.
- Stop conditions prevent over-messaging and protect your sender reputation.
- AI can generate each step if you provide a brief prompt describing the angle you want.
- Follow-up emails should generally be shorter and punchier than the initial outreach message.
- Sequences respect the campaign send windows and daily limits you configure.
