# How to View Campaign Details and Performance

**Last Update:** July 2026

**Post ID:** 135

Once a campaign is running, you need visibility into what's actually happening. Are leads opening? Are they replying? Are emails bouncing? Understanding how to read campaign details coldaf dashboards helps you optimize in real time rather than flying blind. ColdAF provides a full view cold email campaign performance suite so you can drill into metrics, spot trends, and make data-backed decisions. In this guide, we'll show you how to view campaign metrics cold outreach teams rely on to improve results week over week.

## You can view campaign details and performance in just a few steps. Here's how to do it:

1. On your **Dashboard** page, click on **Campaigns** in the left sidebar and in the **Campaigns** panel that opens, click on the campaign name you want to review.
2. In the **Campaign Details** page, review the **Overview** tab (sent, opened, replied, clicked) for a high-level snapshot.
3. Click the **Leads** tab to see per-lead status including pending, sent, opened, replied, bounced, and unsubscribed.
4. Click the **Emails** tab to see the full sent email list with timestamps and tracking status for each message.
5. Click the **Analytics** tab for charts showing volume and engagement trends over time.
6. Use the **Date Range** filter at the top to zoom in on a specific window, and click any metric to drill down into the underlying records.

That's it! Now you can monitor performance and iterate quickly to improve your outreach results.

## Notes

- The **Overview** tab shows real-time metrics that refresh every few minutes.
- The **Leads** tab shows individual status so you can follow up manually with high-interest prospects.
- The **Emails** tab shows each sent email with timestamp and open/click tracking status.
- The **Analytics** tab includes charts for volume and engagement over time, useful for spotting trends.
- Data refreshes every few minutes, so slight delays are normal.
- Clicking any metric in the **Overview** tab will filter the underlying list to show only those records.
